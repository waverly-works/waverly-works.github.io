Create Reminder

Vibrate Alerts
This determines how often the physical Task Tone product will vibrate in reminder
App Alerts

Streak Goal
Including a 'Streak Goal' option lets users decide if the reminder has a streak, motivating them to complete possible tasks

Based on feedback, setting a times via dropdowns can be frustrating, so I added a typing feature

Calendar & View Reminder

View & Create Reminders
After creating a reminder, it will appear in the calender beneath 'View Reminders.' Clicking on a day will show the reminders for that day
Edit Reminders
Editing reminders is easy: clicking on the three dots shows a simple dropdown

Task Tone Warning Faces

Three Stages

1.) Normal, smiling face: not incomplete task

2.) Yellow, sad face: 5 minutes over task

3.) Orange, sad face: 10 minutes over task

Extra Push
Having two additional push notifications for a reminder once it remains incompleted is the extra nudge people need to complete it
Extra Motivation
Because users reported struggling with motivation, the personification of the app will help motivate

Pain Points & Inspiration

Mom - Hearing Loss üëÇ

My mom sometimes forgets to take her insulin shot every night. Due to her being deaf in one ear, auditory alerts don't work for her because she finds them more troublesome. Additionally, she often can't find her phone, much less hear auditory reminders.

Dad - Easy to Ignore üí§

My dad often ignores alarms on his phone, as they're easy to not hear or click stop. He also mentions how snoozing an alarm is kind of like having a 'second' reminder, so to add onto that feature: could there be a feature that pushes reminders after the initial time?

Friend - Lack of Motivation üò™

A friend I interviewed emphasized how it can be easy to ignore reminders if there's no true motivation to keep up the task it wants you to complete.

Friend - Lack of Phone üì±

A friend I interviewed also mentioned how she doesn't have her phone on her constantly, so reminders that are purely cellular are not as useful as they could be.

Problems & Solutions

üí§ EASY TO IGNORE OR SNOOZE
With one press or swipe, a reminder notification can be ignored (snoozed, or stopped)
SOLUTION #1
Users cannot snooze or stop a reminder with one motion
üò™ NO MOTIVATION
With easy-to-ignore reminders, comes a lack of motivation and deadline sensitivity
SOLUTION #2
Users can customize how 'easy' it is to ignore a reminder
üëÇ INACCESSIBLE CUES
Reminders often rely on auditory cues, which are not accessible for broader audiences
SOLUTION #3
Reminders can have both auditory and visual cues
üì± INACCESSIBLE FORMS
For reminders to have more access, they must take on forms outside of only cellphones
SOLUTION #4
This product will have a mobile app and portable physical form
